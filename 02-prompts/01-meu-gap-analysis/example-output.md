# Example Output - MEU Gap Analysis

## Input used

```
- Current MEU base: 1,600,000 users
- MEU target: 2,000,000 users by end of Q4
- MEU gap: 400,000 users (25% above current base)
- Lifecycle breakdown: Acquisition 20%, Activation 15%, Retention 45%, Resurrection 20%
- Recent trend: flat over last 3 months
- Current active campaigns: monthly payday sale event, daily breakfast deals, new user onboarding voucher
```

## Output

**Gap diagnosis**

A 25% gap with a flat 3-month trend signals the existing campaign mix is maintaining the base but not growing it. The high Retention share (45%) suggests the platform has a solid active core, but that core is not expanding - new users are not activating at scale and lapsed users are not returning. With Q4 as the deadline, the gap is too large to close through optimization alone.

**Priority lifecycle stage: Resurrection**

Rationale: Resurrection users (20% of base, ~320,000 users) are the fastest path to MEU recovery. They have already transacted before, so the friction to re-engage is lower than Acquisition. With a 1-6 month lapse window still open, a well-timed offer with urgency can bring a meaningful portion back within the quarter. Targeting Resurrection also does not require the lead time that Acquisition campaigns need (paid media ramp-up, new user onboarding flow).

**Risk flag**

If the flat trend continues for another 4-6 weeks without a Resurrection push, a portion of the lapsed segment will cross the 6-month mark and become significantly harder to re-engage, effectively shrinking the addressable pool mid-quarter.

**Recommended interventions**

1. Resurrection re-engagement campaign - time-limited offer (7-14 day window), deep discount (>=30%), push + email sequence targeting users lapsed 1-5 months. Expected contribution: 80,000-120,000 MEU.
2. Activation push for registered non-transacting users - low minimum spend, easy first transaction mechanic (collect-to-redeem or instant cashback). Expected contribution: 40,000-60,000 MEU.
3. Retention frequency booster - weekly repeated mechanic for active users at risk of dropping below monthly transaction threshold. Defend existing base while growing net MEU.
4. Upgrade monthly payday event from Medium to Big scale for Q4 months - deeper deals, broader channel activation to drive incremental engagement from current base.
5. Review new user onboarding voucher CVR - if Activation is only 15% of base, onboarding may be leaking. Fix before scaling Acquisition spend.
