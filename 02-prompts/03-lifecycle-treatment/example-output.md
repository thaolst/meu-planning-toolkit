# Example Output - Lifecycle Treatment

## Input used

```
- Priority stage: Resurrection
- Platform type: fintech / digital wallet
- Available mechanics: voucher / loyalty points / challenge/streak / referral / push notification / email / in-app banner
- Budget constraint: moderate
```

## Output

### Resurrection (Priority)

**Deal structure**
- Discount depth: 30-40% (needs to feel meaningful after absence)
- Minimum spend: low - match or below the user's last transaction amount
- Cap: standard per user, time-limited (7-14 day expiry creates urgency)

**Best mechanic: time-limited voucher + push + email sequence**
A single deep voucher with a hard expiry delivered via push (day 1) and email reminder (day 5) outperforms open-ended offers for this stage. Resurrection users need a reason to act now, not eventually.

**Communication angle**
"We saved something for you" or "Your [reward/offer] expires in X days" - personalized, not generic. Reference their last transaction category if possible to lower friction back to a familiar behavior.

**Leading indicator to track**
First transaction within 14 days of receiving the offer (not MEU itself - that lags by a month). If day-14 CVR on the offer is below 15%, the deal structure or channel is wrong.

**Never do at this stage**
Do not send a generic mass offer with no expiry. Resurrection users who receive an open-ended discount often bookmark it and never use it. Urgency is the mechanic.

---

### Retention

**Deal structure**
- Discount depth: 15-25% (frequency matters more than depth here)
- Minimum spend: at or slightly above average transaction value to nudge AOV
- Cap: per transaction, not per user - allows multiple uses to build habit

**Best mechanic: challenge/streak**
Retention users are already transacting. The goal is increasing frequency, not triggering a first action. A 7-day or 30-day streak mechanic (transact X times, unlock reward) is more effective than a one-off voucher because it creates forward commitment.

**Communication angle**
Progress framing: "3 of 7 days complete" or "You are halfway to your reward." Retention users respond to momentum, not urgency.

**Leading indicator to track**
Transaction frequency in the 30 days after campaign enrollment vs. 30 days before. A 10%+ increase in frequency is a signal the mechanic is working before MEU numbers confirm it.

**Never do at this stage**
Do not offer a deep discount on a single transaction. It trains users to wait for deals rather than transact habitually, which degrades long-term retention.

---

### Activation

**Deal structure**
- Discount depth: 40-50% (first transaction needs to feel like a no-brainer)
- Minimum spend: as low as possible - remove all friction
- Cap: generous - do not let a low cap be the reason the first transaction fails

**Best mechanic: instant cashback or collect-to-redeem on first transaction**
Activation users have registered but not yet transacted. The barrier is not awareness - it is inertia and lack of trust. Instant reward on first transaction (cashback visible immediately) removes the "what do I get out of this?" hesitation.

**Communication angle**
"Complete your first transaction and get X instantly" - clear, transactional, no ambiguity about what happens next.

**Leading indicator to track**
First transaction completion rate within 7 days of receiving the activation push. Industry benchmark: 20-35% for a well-structured activation offer.

**Never do at this stage**
Do not require multiple steps or a streak to unlock the reward. Activation users will not complete a 7-day challenge. They need a single low-effort action with an immediate visible payoff.

---

### Acquisition

**Deal structure**
- Discount depth: 40-50% on a popular, low-risk category
- Minimum spend: zero or very low for the first transaction
- Cap: per user, one-time

**Best mechanic: referral + new user onboarding voucher**
Referral brings in pre-qualified users (friend vouches for the platform) at lower CAC than paid social. Pair with a strong onboarding voucher for the first transaction in a category with high repeat rate (utilities, food, transport).

**Communication angle**
Social proof from the referrer + platform benefit - "Your friend [name] uses [platform] for [category]. Get [X] off your first transaction."

**Leading indicator to track**
Day-7 and day-30 retention of acquired users (not just install or registration). If day-30 retention is below 20%, the acquisition channel is bringing in low-intent users.

**Never do at this stage**
Do not spend Acquisition budget on users who are unlikely to repeat. Front-load the onboarding voucher in a category with natural repeat purchase behavior, not a one-time high-value transaction.
